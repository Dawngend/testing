Research & Explanation Briefing for the Team

Alright, let's get Team CRYteria set up to win the ACM Techsprint. Transitioning from a baseline RAG pipeline to an adaptive, mobile-friendly study companion requires a shift from a "stateless document reader" to a "stateful, context-aware tutor." Here is the core strategy you can share with the team to explain why and how we are refactoring the codebase:

1. Solving the Bilingual (Taglish) Requirement Language models often default to either formal, rigid English or archaic, textbook Tagalog ("malalim na Tagalog"). To effectively tutor Filipino students, we need to enforce a conversational "Taglish" tone—the natural way students actually speak. We achieve this by moving away from generic prompt instructions and instead injecting a strict persona and formatting rules into the system prompt within generator.py, explicitly instructing the Llama 3.3 model on how to blend the languages without losing technical accuracy.

2. Solving the Adaptive Practice Exercises A standard RAG just retrieves text. An Adaptive RAG tracks the user. We will update database.py (SQLite) to log a student's grade_level and an evolving mastery_score. When a user asks a question, rag_engine.py will pull this state and pass it as metadata to ChromaDB. This ensures the retrieved context matches their level. Then, generator.py will use Groq's structured JSON output capabilities to force the LLM to generate an array of exercises dynamically scaled to their current mastery.

3. Solving Mobile Optimization & Low-Bandwidth Constraints Streamlit is great for rapid prototyping, but its default behavior is to re-run the entire script upon every interaction. On a spotty data connection, re-running a heavy pdfplumber or pytesseract extraction will crash the app or drain the user's load. We will implement aggressive caching (@st.cache_data and @st.cache_resource) in extractor.py and app.py. This means once a document is OCR'd, the result lives in the local device memory. We will also utilize Streamlit's layout containers to ensure the UI stacks vertically and scales fluidly for mobile screens.
